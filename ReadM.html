    Community Midwives

    Community midwifery involves providing maternal and newborn care in the community setting. Midwives work with women before, during, and after childbirth, focusing on providing care in homes or community health centers , especially in underserved or rural areas. They also emphasize prenatal education, breastfeeding support, and postpartum care.

    üìå Skills and Qualities for a Community Midwife

    • Empathy and Compassion:

      need to support families through the stress and excitement of childbirth with kindness.

    • Clinical Knowledge:

      A solid understanding of maternal and newborn health, and the ability to recognize complications early.

    • Communication Skills:

      Good midwives are excellent communicators, able to explain procedures and provide education to families.

    • Problem-Solving Abilities:

      Community midwives need to make decisions quickly, especially when dealing with complications in remote settings.

    üéØ Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA)
